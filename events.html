September 23rd, 2025 (Tuesday)

Second MSB EMERGE Workshop


The MSB EMERGE workshop is a half-day workshop fully organized by students and designed for students to present their research and support them in their journey to become independent investigators and mentors. We encourage early-career researchers - maximum two years post Ph.D. - to be primary investigators and occupy the first and/or last author positions. Another unique feature of our workshop is the dedicated panel of experts who will engage in discussion with the presenters to provide constructive feedback on extending their work to a journal submission. We publish the accepted papers in Springer LNCS workshop proceedings and provide an invitation to submit as full extended papers in MELBA journal. This initiative also aligns with one of our primary objectives to enhance diversity within our community further. For more details, visit: https://miccaimsb.github.io/emerge/.




September 24th/25th/26th, 2025 (Wednesday, Thursday and Friday)

Doctoral Consortium (Poster Presentation)


Posters from Doctoral Consortium participants—highlighting the core ideas of their doctoral research—will be on display throughout the main conference. These posters offer a unique opportunity for attendees to engage in thoughtful discussions with emerging researchers. The submissions will be featured in the MICCAI Emerging Scholars Collection, which will be hosted on the MSB website and the MICCAI Society platform.







September 25th/26th, 2025 (Thursday and Friday)

Morning Runs


Kickstart your day with a breathtaking sunrise run along Daejeon's scenic riverside. It's a wonderful opportunity to connect with fellow MICCAI attendees, invigorate your spirit, and set the pace for the day. Lace up your running shoes, bring your smiles, register, and join us in welcoming the new day! All fitness levels are welcome.









September 24th, 2025 (Wednesday @ Evening)

Academia and Industry Panel and Networking Event


The MSB has a goal to provide opportunities for professional development and networking. The A&I event educates current students on career possibilities post graduation, and provides students a venue to ask questions related to professional development. The A&I event will begin at 6 PM on the first day of the main conference with a one-hour moderated panel discussion on career development and the differences between academic and corporate research. Panelists are selected to represent a diverse set of backgrounds, perspectives and experiences. The second hour of the A&I event will feature a networking session structured in a speed-dating style.






September 25th, 2025 (Thursday @ Lunch)

MSB Lunch, MICCAI Educational Challenge and PhD Thesis Madness


Videos of the top-6 submissions to the MICCAI Educational Challenge will be shown during the MSB Lunch event. The MICCAI Educational Challenge is a competition that aims to promote the development of educational resources in the field of medical imaging and computer-assisted interventions.
The best doctoral consortium submissions will be invited to present their work in a 3-minute oral presentation during the MSB Lunch event. The Thesis Madness event allows final-year Ph.D. students and freshly finished doctorates to showcase their groundbreaking work in a short framework. The event will take place during the lunch and will offer the opportunity for upcoming researchers to connect, collaborate, and construct new ideas.
Lunch will be served at the venue.



September 26th, 2025 (Friday @ Evening)

MICCAI Soccer Game


The traditional MICCAI soccer game will return this year! It will take place on two courts for 5-vs-5. The game will be hosted at 6:30 P.M. on September 26th evening. Participants will be guided from the conference center to the nearby court.

LIMITED SEATS, REGISTRATION REQUIRED. Now OPEN on the main conference registration portal.







September 27th, 2025 (Saturday @ Evening)

Evening Tour: Taste & Traditions - An Evening of Korean Flavors


Start with food tastings at a traditional Korean market, including popular street snacks. Try your hand at nostalgic Korean games like Dalgona and Ddakji (some made popular by Squid Game). Then enjoy a relaxed dinner with local Korean dishes, followed by a stroll down Sky Road, a well-known pedestrian street with lights, performances, and shops. Wrap up the evening with some café-hopping in the area. Includes dinner, a small gift from the famous Sung Sim Dang Bakery, transportation, and travel insurance. Participants will be transported to and from the event.

LIMITED SEATS, REGISTRATION REQUIRED. Now OPEN on the main conference registration portal.




September 28th, 2025 (Sunday)

Full-Day Guided Tour: Discover Daejeon's Science & Heritage


Explore Daejeon through a full-day guided tour covering science, history, and scenic views. Stops include the National Science Museum, Dongchundan Historical Park, Uam Historical Park, and Daedong Sky Park with an English-speaking guide. Enjoy hands-on exhibits, stories of Korea’s scholarly past, and sweeping city views. The tour includes a traditional kalguksu lunch, morning snacks, a cultural gift, transportation, and travel insurance. Participants will be transported to and from the event.

LIMITED SEATS, REGISTRATION REQUIRED. Now OPEN on the main conference registration portal.
